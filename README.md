## 💡 Overview

<a href="#outputArcade">Output Arcade</a> serves music producers, beatmakers, sound designers, and musicians who want a fresh approach to sample-based music production — an instrument that makes loops playable and manipulable in ways that standard loop libraries and samplers don't provide.

The <a href="#outputArcade">beatmaker and hip-hop producer use case</a> uses Arcade's chop mode and pitched playback to create melodic and rhythmic elements from sample content — the chopped vocal, the pitched drum texture, the layered sample bed that characterizes contemporary beat production. Arcade's approach to samples matches the creative process of producers who build from sample manipulation. The <a href="#outputArcade">electronic music producer workflow</a> uses Arcade's layering and real-time manipulation for textural sound design — building evolving, filtered, pitched loop textures that serve as pads, backgrounds, and transitional elements in electronic music production.

The <a href="#outputArcade">subscription content model</a> means Arcade users receive new sample content continuously — rather than buying a fixed library that becomes familiar over time, Arcade subscribers access an expanding library with new kits and loops added frequently. Daily new content provides ongoing inspiration without additional purchases. <a href="#outputArcade">Output's sound design quality</a> reflects the company's reputation in the music production community — Output builds instruments and sample libraries specifically for professional music production contexts, and Arcade's content curation reflects that professional focus.

<a href="#outputArcade">Remix and variation tools</a> generate variations on the current kit setup — randomizing loop selections and settings within a coherent musical context for rapid exploration of different sound directions from a single starting point. <a href="#outputArcade">User sample import</a> loads personal audio files into Arcade's engine — applying Arcade's pitched playback, chop mode, and manipulation capabilities to original recordings and custom samples rather than only the built-in library content.

<a href="#outputArcade">Standalone and plugin modes</a> — Arcade runs as a standalone application for sound exploration and performance outside a DAW context, and as an AUv3, AU, or VST plugin within a DAW for integrated music production. <a href="#outputArcade">Mac and iOS availability</a> brings Arcade to iPhone and iPad for mobile music creation, with the same cloud library and sound engine available on Apple mobile devices through the iOS version.
